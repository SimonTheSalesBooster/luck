# Luck: Find What's Choking Your Sales and Fix It This Week

> For B2B business owners who want to close bigger deals, faster, without hiring more people.

You don't need more leads. You don't need a bigger team. You need to find the ONE thing choking your sales system and fix it before touching anything else.

Some businesses turn 1 in 50 conversations into deals. Others turn 1 in 5. Same effort. Wildly different results. The difference isn't talent. It's knowing where your system breaks.

This is your diagnostic. Run it. Find your constraint. Fix it in your next sprint.

---

## How It Works

7 checkpoints. Work through them in order. Stop at the first one that fails. That's your constraint.

Fix it this sprint. Then run the diagnostic again next Monday. The next constraint will show up.

**Do NOT skip ahead.** Fixing checkpoint 5 while checkpoint 2 is broken is like optimizing your email subject lines when your offer doesn't match what the market wants. Wasted sprint.

## Checkpoint 1: Solvency

**Can your business survive a quiet month?**

If you stop selling for 30 days, what happens? If revenue drops to near zero, you don't have a business. You have a job where you're both the boss and the employee.

**Red flags:**
- Revenue resets every month
- Pipeline empties when you stop filling it
- Every deal needs you in the room to close
- There's no recurring revenue covering your basics

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Calculate your "silence revenue" (what comes in if you do nothing for 30 days)
- [ ] If it's below 40% of monthly costs, build a recurring offer this sprint
- [ ] Document your sales process so someone (or an AI agent) could run the first call without you
- [ ] Separate who finds the deal from who delivers the work

**Monday strategy review:** What's our silence revenue number? What's the plan to raise it?
**Friday results review:** Did we add any recurring revenue this week? What's the new floor?

## Checkpoint 2: Gradient

**Are you selling into real pain, or nice-to-have improvement?**

Pain creates urgency. Urgency creates deals. If your prospect's problem is "we should probably improve our sales process someday," you'll chase them for months. If it's "we lost 3 deals this month and I don't know why," they'll chase you.

**Red flags:**
- Prospects say "interesting" but don't buy
- Sales cycles drag on for months
- Deals stall at proposal stage
- You compete on price because nobody feels urgency

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Review your last 10 sales conversations. Count how many prospects used emotional language (frustrated, losing sleep, bleeding money). If fewer than 6 out of 10, your gradient is weak
- [ ] Rewrite your opening pitch around the cost of inaction: "What happens if this isn't fixed in 90 days?"
- [ ] Add a disqualification question early: "On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is this?" Below 7? Move on.
- [ ] Walk away from low-urgency prospects. They cost you deals from high-urgency ones

**Monday strategy review:** What's the urgency score of every prospect in our pipeline?
**Friday results review on sprint dashboard:** How many conversations had real urgency this week? Track the ratio.

## Checkpoint 3: Compatibility

**Can prospects actually say yes to your offer?**

You might solve a real problem. The pain might be real. But if your offer doesn't fit how they buy, they won't convert. This is the gap between "I want this" and "I can actually do this right now."

**Red flags:**
- High attention, low conversion (they see you, they don't buy)
- Prospects say "love it" then disappear
- Huge price gaps in your offer ladder (no stepping stones)
- Prospects can't explain what you do to their business partner in one sentence

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Map your offer ladder. If there's a 10x+ jump between tiers (like $49 to $900 or $900 to $27,000), build a bridge offer this sprint
- [ ] Write a one-sentence description of your offer that a prospect could text to their co-founder. If you can't, simplify
- [ ] Reduce first-purchase risk: pilot project, money-back guarantee, "pay after results"
- [ ] Ask 3 recent prospects who didn't buy: "What would have made this a yes?" Listen for structural problems, not objections

**Monday strategy review:** Where are prospects getting stuck in our buying path?
**Friday results review:** How many proposals sent vs. signed? What's the conversion gap telling us?

## Checkpoint 4: Niche Construction

**Does every client make the next one easier to get?**

Linear businesses grow by grinding: one client, then another, same effort every time. Compounding businesses grow by building loops: every client generates proof, referrals, and network effects that make the next client cheaper.

**Red flags:**
- Client acquisition cost stays flat or rises
- Past clients don't refer you
- You have alumni who generate zero revenue
- Your content gets attention but not leads
- Nobody talks about you when you're not in the room

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Build a referral trigger into your delivery process (not "please refer me" but a structural moment: a graduation call, a co-created case study, a warm intro request at peak satisfaction)
- [ ] Turn your best client result into a public asset this sprint: video testimonial, case study, joint LinkedIn post
- [ ] Send one reactivation email to your alumni this week: "Here's what's changed since we worked together. Here's what you're probably leaving on the table."
- [ ] Count: what % of your last 20 clients came from referrals or returning buyers? Below 30% means this is your constraint

**Monday strategy review:** What referral mechanics are built into our current delivery?
**Friday results review on sprint dashboard:** How many referrals or reactivations came in this week?

## Checkpoint 5: Circulation

**Does value flow through your system and come back?**

This is the silent killer. You do everything "right." Content goes out. Clients pay. Episodes air. But nothing comes back. Everything flows outward. Growth stays flat. You feel busy and stuck at the same time.

**Red flags:**
- Massive content output, no measurable pipeline impact
- Clients finish and disappear
- Email list grows but conversion stays flat
- You have a large audience that doesn't buy
- Revenue is almost 100% from new clients, never from existing ones

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Audit every piece of content you produce this week: does it have a clear next step for the reader/viewer? If not, add one or stop producing it
- [ ] Design the "after" for every offer: when a client finishes this engagement, what's the natural next thing?
- [ ] Build a re-engagement sequence for past clients: not "buy again" but "here's what's new and here's your next opportunity"
- [ ] Track your return rate: what % of revenue comes from existing relationships (upsells, renewals, referrals)? Below 40% = circulation constraint

**Monday strategy review:** Where is energy going out of our system and not coming back?
**Friday results review on sprint dashboard:** Return rate this week. What % of revenue came from existing relationships?

## Checkpoint 6: Integration

**Do the parts of your business reinforce each other?**

Your podcast should feed your email list. Your email list should feed your pipeline. Your pipeline should feed your case studies. Your case studies should feed your podcast. When these connections are dense, everything amplifies everything. When they're siloed, each channel costs full effort on its own.

**Red flags:**
- Your content topics don't match what prospects ask in sales calls
- Your CRM, email, calendar, and content don't talk to each other
- Adding a new channel means adding new effort (no leverage from existing channels)
- Removing one channel wouldn't affect any other channel

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Record 3 sales calls this week. Write down every question and objection. Those are your content topics for next sprint
- [ ] Build one automation this sprint that connects two currently siloed systems (example: podcast episode automatically becomes a newsletter, which automatically feeds your CRM)
- [ ] Use AI to bridge gaps: meeting transcripts to follow-up emails, client results to outreach templates, sales objections to content topics
- [ ] Map your systems on a whiteboard. Draw lines between everything that's connected. Where there are no lines, that's your integration gap

**Monday strategy review:** Which two systems are most disconnected right now?
**Friday results review:** Did we build at least one new connection between systems this sprint?

## Checkpoint 7: Timing

**Is the market ready for what you're selling right now?**

Same offer, different timing, completely different results. During disruptions (like AI reshaping B2B sales right now), the window for positioning is short. Being early looks exactly like being wrong, until suddenly it doesn't.

**Red flags:**
- Your offer feels "nice to have" in the current climate
- Competitors with worse offers are winning because they ride the current wave
- You're selling the same way you sold 2 years ago
- Prospects don't feel urgency because the status quo still works for them

**Sprint fix:**
- [ ] Reframe your offer around the disruption your market is living through RIGHT NOW. If AI is changing how your clients sell, make that the hook
- [ ] Position at the intersection of fear and hope: "Everything is changing. Here's how you win."
- [ ] Ship your new positioning this sprint. Don't polish. The market rewards presence over perfection during disruptions
- [ ] Test one new angle this week: a cold email, a LinkedIn post, a podcast episode. Track if prospects lean forward or lean back

**Monday strategy review:** What's happening in our market right now that creates urgency we can speak to?
**Friday results review:** Did our new framing get a different response than our old one?

## The 7 Failure Patterns (Quick Reference)

| Pattern | Feels Like | Actual Problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Treadmill** | "I have to sell every month just to survive" | No recurring revenue floor | Build solvency |
| **The Vitamin Seller** | "People are interested but nobody buys" | Selling improvement, not pain relief | Reposition around urgency |
| **The Beautiful Misfit** | "Everyone loves it but nobody signs" | Offer doesn't fit how they buy | Redesign the buying path |
| **The Invisible Engine** | "Clients love us, but new ones don't find us" | No compounding loops | Build referral mechanics into delivery |
| **The Leaky Bucket** | "We do so much but growth is flat" | Value goes out, nothing comes back | Build return paths |
| **The Silo Farm** | "We have great parts that don't connect" | Low integration | Connect existing systems |
| **The Time Traveler** | "Our offer is great, timing is wrong" | Misaligned with current disruption | Reframe for right now |

## 5-Minute Diagnostic

Answer honestly. Stop at the first No.

1. Does 40%+ of my revenue continue if I stop selling for 30 days? → No? **Fix solvency**
2. Do 6/10 prospects describe their problem with real emotion? → No? **Fix gradient**
3. Can a stranger become a client in 3 clear, low-risk steps? → No? **Fix compatibility**
4. Do 30%+ of clients come from referrals or returning buyers? → No? **Fix niche construction**
5. Is 40%+ of revenue from existing relationships? → No? **Fix circulation**
6. Would removing one channel hurt all the others? → No? **Fix integration**
7. Do prospects lean forward when I describe what I do? → No? **Fix timing**

Your first No is your binding constraint. Put it on your sprint dashboard. Fix it this sprint.

## AI Acceleration (No Hiring Required)

You don't need more people. You need AI working on your actual constraint.

| Constraint | What AI Does For You |
|---|---|
| **Solvency** | AI agents handle follow-ups, renewals, and upsells so revenue doesn't depend on you being available |
| **Gradient** | AI analyzes your sales calls and extracts the exact words prospects use to describe their pain. Use their language, not yours |
| **Compatibility** | AI builds and A/B tests bridge offers, landing pages, and sequences until you find what converts |
| **Niche Construction** | AI turns every client conversation into case studies, testimonials, and referral triggers automatically |
| **Circulation** | AI creates return paths: alumni reactivation flows, content-to-CTA pipelines, post-engagement nurture sequences |
| **Integration** | AI connects your systems: transcripts to CRM, episodes to newsletters, objections to content |
| **Timing** | AI monitors competitors and market shifts so you reposition before the window closes |

## Sprint Cadence for Constraint Fixing

**Monday Strategy Review:**
- Run the 5-minute diagnostic (or review last week's constraint)
- Identify the binding constraint for this sprint
- Set 2-3 specific actions on the sprint dashboard

**Daily (5 min):**
- Are today's activities aimed at the binding constraint, or am I avoiding it by working on something easier?

**Friday Results Review:**
- Did the binding constraint improve? Show the numbers
- Sprint dashboard: what moved, what didn't, what's next
- If the constraint improved, run the diagnostic again. Find the next one.

**Every 90 days:**
- Full diagnostic. All 7 checkpoints. Where are we now vs. 90 days ago?
- Celebrate what shifted. Name what's next.
